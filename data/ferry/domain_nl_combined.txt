--- TYPE HIERARCHY ---



--- PREDICATES ---

  {?x} is not equal to {?y}
  {?c} is a car
  {?l} is a location
  {?l} is at the ferry
  {?c} is at {?l}
  the ferry is empty
  {?c} is on
  {?x1} is equal to {?x2}
  {?x1} is not equal to {?x2}


--- ACTIONS ---

  sail a location A to a location B
    I can only sail a location A to a location B if it is the case that A is not equal to B and A is a location and B is a location and A is at the ferry
    Once I sail a location A to a location B, it is the case that B is at the ferry
    Once I sail a location A to a location B, it is not the case anymore that A is at the ferry

  board a car A from a location B onto the ferry
    I can only board a car A from a location B onto the ferry if it is the case that A is a car and B is a location and A is at B and B is at the ferry and the ferry is empty
    Once I board a car A from a location B onto the ferry, it is the case that A is on
    Once I board a car A from a location B onto the ferry, it is not the case anymore that A is at B and it is not the case anymore that the ferry is empty

  debark a car A at a location B
    I can only debark a car A at a location B if it is the case that A is a car and B is a location and A is on and B is at the ferry
    Once I debark a car A at a location B, it is the case that A is at B and the ferry is empty
    Once I debark a car A at a location B, it is not the case anymore that A is on
